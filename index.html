About AMD:

AMD is a modern day computing company based in Santa Clara, California that developes computer processers, computer accessories, and many real world technological advancements.


Originally, AMD was founded by Jerry Sanders, Jack Gifford, John Carey, Larry Stenger, Frank Botte, Edwin Turney, Sven Simonsen, Jim Giles on May 1, 1969.

AMD Timeline

1972
The company goes public and shortly thereafter begins to make computer chips. 		 
  1982
AMD begins supplying computer chips to IBM.
1991
AMD begins selling a new microprocessor named the Am386.		  
  1994
U.S. Supreme Court rules in favor if Intel over a microprocessor legal dispute between the two major companies.
1996
AMD buys the company NexGen. 		 
  2000
The AMD Atholon processor is developed. This is a step in the right direction for AMD.
2003
AMD announces the new Opteron chip (this chip is used in server machines).		  
  2006
AMD acquires ATI Technologies, which allows them to devlop graphics processing units and other technological hardware goods
2008
The company begins the process of splitting itself in half.		  
  2009
Intel wins in a $1.45 billion violation of anittrust laws
2014
AMD now makes CPU's GPU's and other computer hardware devices		  

AMD's 5 major milestones:

  1. 1991, the launch of AMD's new microprocessor. The Am386.
  2. 2000, the AMD Athlon processer is developed.
  3. 2003, AMD begins production on the Opteron chip.
  4. 2014, AMD begins selling CPU's and GPU's.
  5. 2021, Amd launches the next gen GPU's that have a leading edge to it's competition (NDIVIA)
